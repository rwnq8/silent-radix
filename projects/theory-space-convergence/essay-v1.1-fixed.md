# THEORY-SPACE CONVERGENCE AS THE SHADOW OF PROCESS

**Author:** [TBD] | **Date:** 2026-07-02 | **Status:** v1.1 — red-team fixes applied

---

The convergence of independently developed scientific theories on identical mathematical structures reveals something genuine about the physical world [operationally: the observable universe as described by our best current theories], but not a pre-existing, Platonic landscape of forms that dictates the shape of physical law.

The "structure" of the space of possible theories — its attractors, fixed points, and basins of convergence — is not a primary cause but a *projection* of a deeper principle: the severe constraint on stable, large-scale patterns that can emerge when many interacting components obey simple, universal micro-physical processes.

These processes — local nonlinear interaction, competition, selection for stability, tension between aggregation and dissipation, deterministic chaos, symmetry-breaking — form a finite alphabet of dynamical motifs. Across vastly different material substrates, this same alphabet generates the same long-wavelength effective theories. The attractor structure of theory-space is precisely the *shadow* that this finite dynamical vocabulary casts onto the space of all possible formal descriptions. It is a map drawn from the territory of self-organizing matter, not an autonomous terrain that matter is forced to follow.

Thus, consilience and convergence are epistemically consequential but ontologically secondary. They testify to the universality of process, not the a priori power of mathematics. The reason scientists meet again and again at the same equations is the same reason disparate physical systems arrive at the same critical exponents: only a sharply restricted family of macroscopic behaviours survives coarse-graining when the underlying logic is chaos harnessed by selection. The topology of theory-space is the *expression* of this pruning, and its apparent depth is the depth of self-organization itself, viewed from the meta-level of description.

## ON THE PRIORITY OF PROCESS OVER STRUCTURE

The shadow metaphor commits this framework to an asymmetric claim: **process is logically prior to the structures it projects.** An object does not "co-arise" with its shadow — the object exists first, and the shadow is its consequence. The structured landscape of consistent theories is the observable geometry of self-organization's inherent robustness. The dynamics of matter — governed by the finite alphabet — are the generative source; the mathematics is the formal trace those dynamics leave behind.

This does NOT imply that mathematical structures are "unreal" or "mere social constructions." The convergent attractors (Lie groups, Riemannian manifolds, ultrametric trees, Bruhat-Tits buildings) are objectively real features of the physical world — as real as universality classes. But their reality is the reality of **patterns**, not of Platonic **forms**. They exist because processes with universal properties inevitably produce them, not because they pre-exist in a timeless realm awaiting discovery.

## FALSIFIABILITY

This position makes concrete predictions: find a stable, coarse-grainable effective theory that CANNOT arise from any combination of the dynamical alphabet, and the thesis is disconfirmed. Conversely, non-convergent theoretical domains (interpretations of quantum mechanics, the string landscape) are explained by the thesis: convergence fails specifically where the dynamical alphabet is not operative — i.e., where theory choice is underdetermined by empirical pruning.

## PHILOSOPHY BOUNDARY

[PHILOSOPHY] The question of what "grounds" the dynamical alphabet itself — whether it is metaphysically necessary, anthropically selected, or derivable from deeper constraints — lies beyond the scope of this essay's empirical claims and is flagged as an open philosophical question.
